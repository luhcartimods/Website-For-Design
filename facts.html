General Facts About Littering

  • Liter takes around 5 Years to decompose
  • Littering harms ecosystems and wildlife, especially through plastic pollution.
  • The economic burden of cleanup reaches millions yearly, diverting funds from essential services.
  • Litter attracts pests and poses health risks due to toxins and foul odors.
  • life suffers greatly from plastic waste entering oceans and waterways.
  • Legal consequences like fines aim to deter littering, but education is vital for lasting change.
  • Litter diminishes scenic beauty, impacting tourism and local economies.
  • Pollution from litter affects soil, water, and air quality, endangering health.
  • Education campaigns foster social responsibility against littering.
  • Recycling and proper waste management are key solutions to reduce litter.
  • Collective action, including personal responsibility and community engagement, is crucial to combat littering effectively.
